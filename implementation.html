Implementation Roadmap

A phased, disciplined 20-year transition to restore British governance, liberty, and prosperity while maintaining stability and national security.

20-Year Implementation Roadmap

Phase 0: Preparation (Year 0–1)

Pass enabling legislation with Jubilee clauses. Establish gold accounting for all government budgets. Conduct baseline measurements for PETS (HOT, LOT, ROT, COT, etc.) and police insurance premiums. Begin biometric infrastructure for the Golden Hurdle. Appoint transitional sheriffs and begin parish moot pilots.

Phase 1: Stabilisation & Quick Wins (Years 1–5)
  • Implement FLIP Phase 1 (pension pivot) for policing.
  • Launch Golden Hurdle immigration system and end new naturalisations.
  • Introduce STORK rebate system with Gold Barrel cohorts.
  • Begin aggressive subsidiarity: restore historic county/borough/parish boundaries.
  • Privatise non-strategic assets via PLUSH (BBC, student loans, etc.).
  • Enact Secure Digital Crime Reporting Act (SDCRA) and Assize of Worth for judges.
  • Freeze welfare in nominal terms and begin wind-down for non-nationals.
Phase 2: Structural Reform (Years 5–10)
  • Full rollout of PETS (Pigouvian Externality Tax Suite) replacing most existing taxes.
  • Complete major privatisations under PLUSH (NHS elements, education, residential streets, etc.).
  • Implement private marriage contract framework and full STORK operation.
  • Transition to Heinleinian franchise: first National Service cohorts complete and gain citizenship.
  • Reform House of Lords with National Service requirement for inheritance.
  • Roll out historic county-based elected sheriffs and local policing independence.
Phase 3: Consolidation & Deep Reform (Years 10–15)
  • Transition police funding to Phase 2 Gold Constabulary Pots.
  • Complete welfare phase-out for working-age nationals (replace with private charity and family responsibility).
  • Deregulate planning and accelerate land privatisation.
  • Embed Jubilee legislative reset process fully.
  • Expand private certification markets to replace remaining regulatory bodies.
Phase 4: Maturation (Years 15–20)
  • Full legacy pension wind-down across public sector.
  • Bank of England privatisation and end of monetary intervention (except wartime).
  • Finalise return to pre-1997 constitutional baseline with new safeguards.
  • Parish and district-level experimentation with voluntary local services.
  • Comprehensive review and repeal of obsolete statutes under Jubilee process.
Ongoing Principles

All major structural changes at local level require two-thirds council vote + binding referendum. Central government changes require Jubilee process. Gold denomination anchors all budgets to prevent inflation of spending. Privatisation via Vickrey auctions ensures fair market pricing. No new entitlements created — only rebates, transitions, and sunset clauses.

The Goal

By Year 20 the United Kingdom will have a minimal, effective, locally accountable state focused solely on national defence and the suppression of serious violence. Most functions will have returned to private hands, markets, communities, and individuals. The nation will be demographically stable, fiscally disciplined, and constitutionally restrained.

Strong where it must be. Absent where it should be.
