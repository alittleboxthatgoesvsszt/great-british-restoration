Great British National Restoration Framework

Implementation Roadmap

A disciplined, phased 20-year transition to restore a lean, effective, and constitutionally restrained British state while maintaining stability and national security.

20-Year Implementation Roadmap

Phase 0: Preparation (Years 0–1)

Pass core enabling legislation with Jubilee clauses. Convert all government accounting to gold ounces. Establish baseline measurements for PETS taxes. Begin biometric infrastructure for the Golden Hurdle. Launch pilot parish moots and prepare historic county restoration.

Phase 1: Stabilisation & Quick Wins (Years 1–5)
  • Implement FLIP Phase 1 for policing accountability
  • Launch the Golden Hurdle immigration system and end new naturalisations
  • Introduce STORK rebate with Gold Barrel cohorts
  • Restore historic county, borough, and parish boundaries
  • Begin major privatisations under PRUNE (BBC, student loans, etc.)
  • Enact SDCRA and Assize of Worth
  • Freeze welfare in nominal terms and begin phase-out for non-nationals
Phase 2: Structural Reform (Years 5–10)
  • Full rollout of PETS replacing most existing taxes
  • Accelerate PRUNE privatisations (NHS elements, education sector, residential streets)
  • Implement full private marriage contract framework
  • First National Service cohorts complete and earn citizenship
  • Reform House of Lords with National Service requirement
  • Roll out elected sheriffs and county-level policing independence
Phase 3: Consolidation (Years 10–15)
  • Transition police to Phase 2 Gold Constabulary Pots
  • Complete welfare normalisation for working-age nationals
  • Full deregulation of planning via property rights and tort law
  • Embed Jubilee legislative reset process
  • Expand private certification to replace legacy regulatory bodies
Phase 4: Maturation (Years 15–20)
  • Complete legacy public sector pension wind-down
  • Privatise the Bank of England and end routine monetary intervention
  • Return to pre-1997 constitutional baseline with new mechanical safeguards
  • Full review and repeal of obsolete statutes under Jubilee process
Ongoing Safeguards

All major local changes require two-thirds council vote + binding referendum. Central changes require the Jubilee process. All budgets remain denominated in gold. Privatisation uses Vickrey auctions. No new open-ended entitlements are created.

The Goal

By Year 20 Britain will have a minimal, highly effective state focused solely on defence and justice. Most functions will have returned to individuals, families, markets, and communities. The nation will be demographically stable, fiscally disciplined, and constitutionally protected against future expansion.
