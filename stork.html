Strategic Tax Offset Rebate for Kids (STORK)

Core Purpose

STORK is not welfare, a subsidy, or a pronatalist bribe. It is a capped tax rebate that partially corrects the economic drag caused by the state’s own taxation and policies on those British parents who are already contributing in kind to the nation’s long-term survival by raising the next generation.

Budget & Negative Feedback Mechanism

The total annual STORK budget is fixed forever at 61,200,000 troy ounces of gold, divided equally among all eligible British children up to age 17.

This creates a gentle negative feedback loop: higher birthrates → smaller per-child rebate; lower birthrates → larger per-child rebate — helping stabilise the national fertility rate toward replacement without open-ended spending.

Gold Barrel System: Each tax year opens a new 17-year cohort barrel receiving exactly 3,600,000 troy ounces annually. Children born in that year draw equally from their cohort’s barrel.

Eligibility

Available only to:

  • British national children
  • Claimed by their natural married parents (or a widowed parent / qualifying blood-relative adoptive parent)

Requires a simple private marriage contract creating joint and several liability for the child until age 18 and designating the child as primary residual heir.

Unmarried parents and parents of non-nationals do not qualify.

How the Wider Framework Supports Fertility

STORK works in concert with the rest of the system:

  • PETS / HOT lowers the overall tax burden and encourages living together, encouraging family formation.
  • PRUNE privatisation and deregulation reduce cost of living across housing, education, and services.
  • Stronger rule of law, lower crime (FLIP), and better environmental outcomes improve quality of life.
  • Controlled immigration (Golden Hurdle) reduces the scope for the ruling classes to artificially supress wages by taxing the public and using it to subsidise the import of labour competition.

Together, these policies remove many of the artificial economic and social penalties on family formation created by the current state.

Common Objections Addressed
  • “It discriminates by marital status” — A simple private marriage contract for liability and inheritance is a minimal, reasonable bar for those intending to raise children.
  • “It discriminates against non-nationals” — The nation is a broad kin group with ownership of its territory. Only those producing new Britons qualify for this strategic offset.
  • “This is just redistribution” — It is a partial rebate for contribution in kind. The fixed budget ensures it never covers the full cost of raising a child.
The Goal

To ameliorate the state’s own suppression of British family formation, secure the demographic foundation required for national continuity and defence, and gently stabilise the birthrate — all while remaining fiscally disciplined through a fixed gold budget and strict eligibility rules.
