Great British National Restoration Framework

Criminal Corrections

The purpose of criminal penalties is deterrence, restitution, and the permanent neutralisation of serious threats to life, limb, and property.

Criminal Corrections Policy

Core Principle

Criminal justice exists solely to protect the innocent by deterring aggression and providing restitution to victims. It is not a rehabilitation service, a therapeutic programme, or a tool for social engineering. The state’s role is narrow: deter where possible, extract restitution where possible, and permanently remove those who remain a clear and ongoing danger.

Why Long-Term Prison Is Rejected

Long-term imprisonment is expensive, often criminogenic, and represents ongoing active management of offenders at taxpayer expense. This violates minarchist principles. We therefore reject warehousing criminals for decades.

Hierarchy of Sanctions

Sanctions escalate strictly according to the need for deterrence and restitution:

  • Restitution and Fines — The primary response. Offenders must compensate victims directly (via wage garnishment, asset seizure, or forced labour if necessary).
  • Corporal Punishment (Flogging) — Used when fines alone are insufficient to deter. Swift, memorable, and extremely low cost.
  • Execution — Reserved for the most serious crimes (murder, rape, treason, violent robbery, etc.) or for incorrigible repeat offenders who have proven they cannot be safely returned to society. The offender’s estate is used to maximise restitution to victims.
Integration with FLIP Justice

This system works seamlessly with FLIP Responsive County Policing. Insurance companies and local communities have strong incentives to push for effective, low-cost sanctions that actually deter rather than subsidise criminality.

The Goal

A corrections system that is honest, deterrent, and fiscally responsible. One that prioritises the protection of the law-abiding public and the rights of victims over the comfort or rehabilitation of those who initiate force.
