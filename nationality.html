Great British National Restoration Framework

Nationality, Citizenship & the Franchise

Restoring a clear, responsible distinction between British nationality (by blood and soil), earned citizenship (rights and duties), and the right to vote.

Nationality, Citizenship and the Franchise

British Nationality

British nationality is defined by descent: a person is a British national if at least one parent is a British national at the time of birth.

All naturalisation pathways are permanently closed. British nationality shall henceforth descend only from at least one British national parent and cannot be deprived under any circumstances.

This affirms that Britain is a specific people with a continuous inheritance — not a hotel or a proposition nation.

British Citizenship

Citizenship is an earned status carrying full civic rights and duties. It is distinct from mere nationality.

Full British citizenship is granted upon successful completion of voluntary National Service (military or approved civilian service) between ages 18 and 39.

Legacy citizens (those who held citizenship before the reform) retain their status.

The Franchise (Voting Rights)

The right to vote is restricted to full British citizens — those who have completed National Service or are legacy citizens.

This follows the Heinleinian principle: those who vote on the use of collective force should have demonstrated a willingness to defend the nation.

British nationals who have not completed service retain all other rights of nationality but do not hold the franchise. Foreign nationals have no voting rights.

National Service

Voluntary National Service is open to all British nationals aged 18–39. Options include:

  • Regular military service
  • Service in a Pioneer Corps (infrastructure, civil defence, conservation, disaster response, etc.)

Successful completion grants full citizenship and the franchise. Servicemen may be called upon for national defence or reconstruction duties.

Interaction with Immigration Policy

The Golden Hurdle system ensures foreign nationals have no pathway to British nationality or citizenship. They may only reside as temporary bonded guests with no access to public funds or political rights.

This maintains a clear, bright line between members of the nation and guests.

The Goal

To restore a duty-based political system in which the franchise is earned through demonstrated commitment to the nation, nationality is preserved by descent, and the state serves the British people rather than replacing them.
