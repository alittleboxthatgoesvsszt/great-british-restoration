Great British National Restoration Framework

Abortion Policy

A pragmatic minarchist position grounded in aggregate value, causal responsibility, and the unavoidably arbitrary nature of legal personhood thresholds.

Abortion in a Minarchist Framework

Core Approach

The state should recognise a legal personhood threshold after which abortion is treated as the killing of a legal person (absent clear justification of self-defence). We propose this threshold at **24 weeks’ gestation**, aligning with long-established medical and legal precedent in Britain.

Before this threshold, the matter is left primarily to individual moral valuation and private arrangements. After this point, the state treats the unborn child as possessing rights that must be weighed under the same principles applied to all persons.

Philosophical Grounding

We apply the same aggregate value / price theory framework used in criminal justice: human life has base value that is modified by behaviour and context. An unborn child at late gestation has high aggregate value to the mother, family, and nation, with no initiation of force against others.

Parents bear causal responsibility for foreseeable dependence once a child exists. Unprotected sex that results in pregnancy creates a situation of dependence that cannot be ended without harm to the dependent party.

Why Evictionism is Incoherent

Evictionism (the argument that the mother may evict the fetus at any time because it is a “trespasser”) fails on multiple grounds:

  • Causal responsibility: The mother (and father) created the situation of dependence through voluntary action. One cannot claim trespass when one has placed the other party in that position.
  • Impossibility of gentle eviction: A fetus cannot be “evicted” without lethal harm, especially after viability. There is no non-lethal equivalent of changing the locks.
  • Contractual duty: By engaging in conduct known to risk creating a dependent human being, the parents enter an implicit duty of care until the child can survive independently or be safely transferred to another guardian.

Evictionism attempts to treat the fetus as a pure aggressor when it is the parents who initiated the chain of causation. This is philosophically inconsistent with a responsibility-based ethics.

Practical Policy

The state sets a clear legal threshold at 24 weeks. Before this point, abortion remains legally available. After this point, it is treated as homicide except in narrow cases of genuine medical necessity to save the mother’s life.

Emphasis is placed on prevention through better economic conditions, stronger families (private marriage contracts), and removing state distortions (STORK, lower taxes, housing reform).

The Goal

To minimise abortion not primarily through coercion, but by restoring economic and cultural conditions that support family formation, while drawing a clear, defensible line on when the state must recognise the rights of the unborn.
